#DailyUI

Daily UI Challenge

I am currently designing my way through a 100 day design challenge called DailyUI. My goal is to continue to challenge myself to surprise and challenge myself as a design-oriented thinker, connect with me on Linkedin.

#001
Sign Up Screen

Day one, I have always liked space and so I wanted to do a few designs around that and this is the first; a sign up screen for a mock space program.

#002
Credit Card Checkout

Day two, most of the credit card forms out there are very plain and monotonous so I wanted to go beyond that and design a credit one that was a little more fun and colorful.

#003
Landing Page

Day three, I decided to switch things up and do a desktop mockup instead of mobile for today's challenge of designing a landing page and I wanted to cover a subject very important to me: saving our ocean.

#004
Calculator

Day four was a fun challenge that required me to look at something I have seen a thousand times before and put my own spin on it.

#005
App Icon

Day five, I wanted to challenge myself and create an app icon entirely from scratch with no pre-made elements. So I made an icon for a mountain biking brand using elements created individually created by me (including the wheel).

#006
User Profile

Day six asked me to design a user profile. I decided to take it in the direction of a mobile social platorm for finding and connecting photographers and customers with potential models for shoots and other model work.

#007
Settings

Day seven, I designed a settings page for a concept pool management application.

#008
404 Page

Nobody likes seeing the 404 page but when a site has a funny/corny 404 page it makes the experience a little less upsetting. So for challenge day eight, I created my own./p>

#009
Music Player

Day nine's challenge was design a media player. I initially started designing a mobile only player but I wanted to challenge myself to create two different variations of it for both mobile and desktop.

#010
Social Share

Day 10, design a social sharing icon. I wanted to go beyond just creating a set of static images showing one or two icon states and try my hand at animation for the button sequence. It ended up being more challenging than I anticipated but once I got going it all fell into place.

#011
Flash Message

Day 11, flash message. I wanted to try another button animation after Challenge Day 10 and I got the perfect chance the next day. I created a simple enter button with a loading sequence and either a success or failure display as the end result. I wanted to do something with a loading sequence but beyond the typical spinning circle and this is my result.

#012
E-Commerce Shop (single item)

Day 12, design a single item e-commerce page. I wanted to do something a little different than a traditional e-commerce page and go for a minimalist and modern feel using white-space and bold color.

#013
Direct Messaging

I want to further challenge myself and create beautiful and accessible designs for every project I do. Even for something small, such as challenge like this, because the internet is a place for everyone and its about time it was designed that way. Because of this, the font used in this project is OpenDyslexic; a font designed to enhance readability for dyslexic users like me.

#014
Countdown Timer

I wanted to go beyond your "traditional" countdown timer and create something I hadn't seen before. So I created a countdown timer to a mystery product launch that uses a barcode as the visualization. Feedback is always appreciated, so let me know what you think.

#015
On/Off Switch

I have been liking the challenge of animating my designs and I knew I wanted to try that again for this challenge; so, I took a spin-off a traditional day-night / on-off switch and animated the button itself to be a changing moon to the sun for off to on.

#016
Pop-Up / Overlay

Day 16 I had a little more creative with today's challenge and created a popup asking users if they wish to become a wizard; just for fun.

#017
Email Receipt

This is a email receipt concept for the brand Trek. I have seen a lot of these challenges posted with half of the image on the challenge board and the other half in the background, which I like a lot. So I challenged myself to incorporate that as well and I am pretty happy with the results.

#018
Analytics Chart

I wanted to create a unique analytics chart that I hadn't seen before but that was still usable. I have always liked how the "doughnut" charts look but I have found that they generally look very similar and not much of a challenge. So I decided to try to switch it up and create a stylized doughnut chart using only sketch and no pre-made elements; then put it on a quick little mockup for a dog walking app.

#019
Leaderboard

I have never designed anything like a leader board before so this was a fun challenge from the start. I spent a lot of time looking at many examples and I learned a lot but I also saw a lot of tightly packed content and neutral color. I wanted to make one that was different and would stand out from the rest; because why shouldn’t the leader board be as colorful as the game?

#020
Location Tracker

The last time I worked with map design was awhile ago while with Bloc when I was first starting my design career. Back then I had limited design knowledge and it took me a couple days to make one. Now with nearly a years worth of design experience behind me and one day for the challenge, the outcome is significantly better and the process more fun.

#021
Home Monitoring Dashboard

I was challenged to design a realistic home monitoring dashboard that was aesthetically pleasing and easy to use. I created a simple yet effective mobile home monitoring dashboard with the features I would use most; the temperature and lighting.

#022
Search

The challenge directions for today were intentionally vague and left a lot up to the designer. So I wanted to go beyond a normal search button design and create a unique interaction animation for it; and this is the result.

#023
Onboarding

I wanted to do a mobile on-boarding sequence because in my past projects I haven't had the chance to do something fun and get creative with on-boarding, especially mobile. This is a three frame on-boarding sequence for a mock mobile weather application.

#024
Boarding Pass

This challenge was uniquely interesting because I haven't had the opportunity to design a boarding pass before so it was a little challenging as well. I spent awhile trying to get the layout and look minimalist but still easily readable but in the end I am happy with how it turned out.

#025
TV App

I haven't had the challenge of designing something for a tv before so this was a little new for me. My main focus was on making sure it would be usable via remote and not have too much information or content crowding up the screen.

#026
Subscribe

I have encountered many subscription forms in my lifetime and the only thing that makes them really stand out to me is the use of interesting imagery and colors. So for this challenge I decided to create my own using a unique image and bright colors

#027
Dropdown

For this dropdown challenge I wanted to do another animation, this time using InVision Studio to aid in the animation process. I wanted to do a dropdown menu that was a little different than your average menu and a pop of color. Overall I am pretty happy with how it turned out and I am excited for more animations and challenges.

#028
Contact Us

I wanted to do something a little more unique than the average contact page so I created a avant-garde type contact page for a modern art brand.

#029
Map

For a previous challenge that called for a location tracker I created a mobile map so for this challenge I decided to do desktop. This map highlights common traffic patterns and users can select things to find nearby to explore. This challenge was a lot more tricky than I thought it was going to be but in the end I am pretty happy with the result.

#030
Pricing

I have always loved snakes and thought they were neat so for the challenge of designing a pricing table I decided to create a table for a mock reptile sanctuary. I really like the final design and I am really happy with how this turned out.
